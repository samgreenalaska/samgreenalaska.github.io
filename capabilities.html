Capabilities

What I execute

Hands-on, end-to-end execution of subsea installation and intervention scopes, from pre-installation readiness through commissioning and contingency operations. The discipline behind each of these transfers directly to Alaska operations across the state, whether the work is remote and high-consequence in the field or office-based engineering in town.

Operations planning & execution leadership

Lead end-to-end planning and execution of offshore subsea operations in accordance with project procedures, safety standards, and regulatory requirements. Ensure equipment readiness, inspection, testing, and system validation before execution. Coordinate offshore crews, ROV teams, contractors, and stakeholders; monitor operational parameters and technical limits; drive troubleshooting and contingency operations; and document activities for quality and reporting.

Subsea tree (XT) installation

Pre-installation validation of pressure tests, XT assembly configuration, ROV readiness, and inspection of the tubing head and tubing head spool. Deploy the XT via drill pipe or wire, execute subsea landing and alignment onto the THD, and verify orientation, VX gasket integrity, and full landout. Perform connector engagement and hydraulic locking via ROV hot stabs, conduct connector and gasket pressure testing to acceptance criteria, install the XT cap, and verify the final configuration across hydraulic and electrical interfaces.

Subsea tree (XT) retrieval

Configure the Eccentric Mechanical Tree Running Tool (eMTRT) and verify the XT interface. Remove and inspect the XT cap, vent production and annulus bores to eliminate trapped pressure, then lock the eMTRT and unlock the XT connector via hydraulic function, with secondary unlock contingency if required. Manage VX gasket release, execute lifting while monitoring overpull and rigging limits, recover the XT to surface, and perform post-retrieval inspection and protective-device install. Includes contingency operations such as hydrate breakout.

Hydrate remediation

Diagnose hydraulic malfunction through pressure cycling, vacuum application, and connector stroke monitoring. Develop Management of Change (MOC) strategies to raise operating pressure limits or redefine the recovery approach. Execute chemical remediation with methanol and MEG injection, flushing, and soak cycles; perform ROV-based mechanical intervention; and analyze field data trends (pressure vs. stroke, soak vs. cycle efficiency) to optimize multi-day recovery campaigns that restore connector functionality.

Hydraulic flying lead (HFL) installation

Deck layout and rigging setup including reels, chute, RDS, buoyancy modules, and clump weights. Inspect cobraheads, MQCs, tubing bundles, and rigging; pre-charge the SFL with hydraulic fluid to prevent seawater ingress. Manage reel deployment with tensioners, cranes, and tuggers to avoid bird-caging or clock-springing, control the lay profile and touchdown, and perform ROV/FLOT subsea stabbing and torqueing of MQCs. Verify connection integrity by torque/turn data and visual confirmation.

Electrical flying lead (EFL) installation

Prepare deployment frames, in-line transformers, and EFL jumpers. Perform ROV site survey, cleaning, and jumper installation, then mate electrical connectors (Nautilus / DigiTron) with correct orientation, latch engagement, and seal integrity, maintaining minimum bend radius throughout. Conduct continuity and insulation-resistance (megger) testing to validate pin-to-pin connectivity, interpret schematics to confirm routing, and complete final verification and test acceptance prior to energization.

Well jumper / flowline: UCON connector

Prepare hubs (cap removal, cleaning, inspection, protective covers) and configure ROV tooling (HCT, SRT, SST, CAT) with function and pressure testing. Assemble the UCON jumper topside with gasket installation and bolting, execute lifting and subsea deployment at correct approach angle and alignment tolerance, and perform hub cleaning and connector stroking. Lock connectors and energize seals via hot-stab hydraulic actuation, conduct seal and pressure testing to acceptance criteria, and execute removal operations including hydrocarbon isolation and controlled recovery.

Well jumper / flowline: KLV connector

Complete readiness activities including connector inspection, seal preparation, and tooling verification. Land and align the jumper onto hubs under ROV guidance, operate KLV clamp connectors with torque tools under turn-count and torque monitoring, and use pull-down tools to achieve hub engagement across installation tolerances. Verify via visual indicators, torque limits, and turn counts; conduct seal and pressure testing; capture installation data for reporting; and troubleshoot misalignment or incomplete locking using engineered contingency procedures.
